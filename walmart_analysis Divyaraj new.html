import pandas as pd

# Load dataset
file_path = r"C:\Users\divya\Downloads\Walmart.csv"  # update if needed
df = pd.read_csv(file_path)

# Clean column names
df.columns = df.columns.str.strip().str.lower().str.replace(' ', '_')

# Handle missing values
if 'weekly_sales' in df.columns:
    df['weekly_sales'] = df['weekly_sales'].fillna(0)

# Convert date column to datetime
if 'date' in df.columns:
    df['date'] = pd.to_datetime(df['date'], errors='coerce', dayfirst=True)

# Drop fully empty rows
df.dropna(how='all', inplace=True)

# Save cleaned data
clean_path = r"C:\Users\divya\Downloads\Walmart_clean.csv"
df.to_csv(clean_path, index=False)
print("Cleaned dataset saved:", clean_path)

import seaborn as sns

holiday_col = 'holiday_flag'  # change if your dataset uses a different name

if holiday_col in df.columns and sales_col in df.columns:
    df['_holiday_num'] = df[holiday_col].astype(int)
    stats = df.groupby('_holiday_num')[sales_col].agg(['count','mean','median','std'])
    print("Holiday vs Non-Holiday weekly sales:\n", stats)

    plt.figure(figsize=(6,4))
    sns.boxplot(x='_holiday_num', y=sales_col, data=df)
    plt.title('Sales: Holiday (1) vs Non-Holiday (0)')
    plt.xlabel('Holiday Flag')
    plt.ylabel(sales_col)
    plt.tight_layout()
    plt.show()
else:
    print("Missing holiday or sales columns.")

4. Key Observations

  • Store performance: Stores such as #20, #4 and #14 dominate total sales, indicating a concentration of revenue in a few sites.
  • Seasonality: The monthly trend shows clear upward spikes in November and December each year, aligning with major holiday shopping periods.
  • Holiday boost: Holiday weeks show higher median and mean weekly sales compared to non-holiday weeks, confirming that special periods positively impact sales.
  • Weak correlation with external factors: The correlation matrix indicates only weak relationships between sales and features like temperature, fuel price, CPI and unemployment suggesting that internal store/seasonal dynamics matter more than broad economic variables.

5. Conclusions & Recommendations

The sales data analysis reveals that Walmart experiences strong season-driven demand, with predictable peaks during holiday months. The revenue is heavily driven by a small number of stores, which suggests optimization opportunities:

  • Inventory planning: Prioritize stocking for top stores ahead of holiday peaks.
  • Marketing strategies: Allocate more promotional resources to the high-performing stores and during the Nov-Dec period.
  • Resource allocation: Consider reducing investment in under-performing stores or improving their performance through targeted actions.

Given the weak correlation with macro-economic variables, focus should remain on store-specific and seasonal initiatives.

Conclusion

The results show strong seasonal trends and clear spikes during holiday weeks. Top stores dominate sales, while external economic variables show weaker influence.
